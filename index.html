The Heart of

Laos is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia, nestled between China, Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand and Myanmar. Bathed by the Mekong River, its landscape of dense jungles, karst plateaus and more than 4,000 river islands makes it one of the most authentic and least-visited corners of Asia.

Traditions

Culture &
Spirituality

Laos is a country where time seems to have stood still. Daily life is deeply tied to the cycle of temples, the seasons of the Mekong and the rituals that have defined Lao identity for centuries.

01 Tak Bat — The Dawn Offering

Every morning before dawn, rows of monks in saffron robes walk silently through the streets of Luang Prabang. Local laypeople kneel on the pavements and place balls of sticky rice into the monks' bowls. This ceremony — the Tak Bat — is not a tourist spectacle: it is an act of Buddhist merit that has taken place uninterrupted for more than 600 years. To participate respectfully, visitors must dress modestly, refrain from flash photography and maintain complete silence.

02 Pi Mai — The Lao New Year

In April, Laos celebrates its new year amid massive water fights, processions of silver Buddha figures and festivals in every town and village. Pi Mai is the moment when the sins of the previous year are washed away — symbolically with water. Buddha statues are ritually purified in elaborate ceremonies, and families gather to share Khao Niao (sticky rice) and pray together at the temples.

03 The Silk of Laos

Silk weaving in Laos is a centuries-old art passed from mothers to daughters. Each ethnic group has its own geometric patterns — the Lao Loum use naga (mythical serpent) motifs, while the highland Lao Soung incorporate flowers and mountain symbols. Traditional wooden looms produce the famous "sinh" (tube skirts) worn by Lao women at ceremonies. The village of Ban Phanom, near Luang Prabang, is renowned for having the most skilled weavers in the country.

04 Mol Lam Music

Mol lam is Laos's traditional music — a form of narrative song combining poetry, humour, romance and social satire. Mol lam singers improvise lyrics in vocal duels accompanied by the khaen, a bamboo reed instrument that produces hypnotic sounds. Declared an Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO, mol lam lives on in weddings, festivals and religious ceremonies. The city of Savannakhet is considered the cradle of modern mol lam.

05 The Secret War (1964–1973)

Laos endured the most intense bombing campaign per capita in human history: 270 million cluster bombs dropped by the United States between 1964 and 1973 as an undeclared part of the Vietnam War. Today, 30% of those bombs failed to detonate — and continue to kill people. The Plain of Jars in Xieng Khuang, with its mysterious 2,000-year-old stone urns, is also one of the most UXO (unexploded ordnance) contaminated places on earth. Visiting the COPE Centre in Vientiane is a perspective-changing experience.

Economy

A country in
transformation

Laos has experienced sustained economic growth since opening to the market in the 1990s. Its economy is built on hydroelectricity — exported mainly to Thailand and China — as well as mining, tourism and agriculture.

Laos–China Railway

Opened in 2021, the high-speed train linking Boten (on the Chinese border) with Vientiane in 3.5 hours has transformed the country's connectivity and accelerated regional trade.

Asia's Battery

With over 70 hydroelectric dams operating and 100 more under construction, Laos aims to become Southeast Asia's leading clean energy exporter by 2030.

Debt to China

Laos's external debt exceeds 120% of its GDP, with China as the main creditor. Managing this debt is the country's greatest economic challenge in the coming decade.

Lao table

Flavours of
the Mekong

Lao cuisine is perhaps the least known in Southeast Asia — and for that reason, the most authentic. The umami depth of padek (fermented fish paste), the heat of chillies and the freshness of wild herbs define every dish.

🍚

Khao Niao

Sticky glutinous rice — the foundation of every Lao meal. Eaten with the hands, rolled into small balls and dipped into sauces.

🥗

Larb

The national dish. Minced meat (buffalo, pork or chicken) with fresh herbs, lime juice, chillies and ground toasted rice.

🥘

Or Lam

A Luang Prabang stew with buffalo, wild eggplant, wood herb and lemongrass. Slow-cooked with deep, complex flavours.

🥖

Khao Jee

The Lao baguette — French colonial heritage filled with pâté, chillies and fresh herbs. An accidental but perfect fusion.

🍜

Khao Piak Sen

Fresh rice noodle soup in chicken or pork broth. Laos's answer to Vietnamese pho — thicker and more comforting.

🍺

Beerlao

The most celebrated lager in Southeast Asia. Golden, smooth and ubiquitous — the unofficial national symbol of Laos.
