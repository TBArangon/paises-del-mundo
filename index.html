Key Facts

The Heart of
Indochina

Laos is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia, nestled between China, Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand and Myanmar. Bathed by the Mekong River, its landscape of dense jungles, karst plateaus and more than 4,000 river islands makes it one of the most authentic and least-visited corners of Asia.

7.5M Population

236K km² land area

Similar in size to Great Britain

Lao Loum, Lao Theung, Lao Soung

1953 Independence

From French colonial rule

Theravada Buddhism — the soul of a nation

65% of the population practises Buddhism. The Tak Bat ceremony — the dawn offering of rice to monks — is a living ritual over 600 years old that takes place every morning in Luang Prabang.

Traditions

Culture &
Spirituality

Laos is a country where time seems to have stood still. Daily life is deeply tied to the cycle of temples, the seasons of the Mekong and the rituals that have defined Lao identity for centuries.

01 Tak Bat — The Dawn Offering

Every morning before dawn, rows of monks in saffron robes walk silently through the streets of Luang Prabang. Local laypeople kneel on the pavements and place balls of sticky rice into the monks' bowls. This ceremony — the Tak Bat — is not a tourist spectacle: it is an act of Buddhist merit practised uninterrupted for over 600 years. To participate respectfully, visitors must dress modestly, avoid flash photography and maintain complete silence.

02 Pi Mai — The Lao New Year

In April, Laos celebrates its new year amid massive water fights, processions of silver Buddha figures and festivals in every town and village. Pi Mai is the moment when the sins of the previous year are washed away — symbolically with water. Buddha statues are ritually purified in elaborate ceremonies, and families gather to share Khao Niao (sticky rice) and pray together at the temples.

03 The Silk of Laos

Silk weaving in Laos is a centuries-old art passed from mothers to daughters. Each ethnic group has its own geometric patterns — the Lao Loum use naga (mythical serpent) motifs, while the highland Lao Soung incorporate flowers and mountain symbols. Traditional wooden looms produce the famous "sinh" (tube skirts) worn by Lao women at ceremonies. The village of Ban Phanom, near Luang Prabang, is renowned for its exceptionally skilled weavers.

04 Mol Lam Music

Mol lam is traditional Lao music — a form of narrative song combining poetry, humour, romance and social satire. Singers improvise lyrics in vocal duels accompanied by the khaen, a bamboo reed instrument with a hypnotic sound. Declared an Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO, mol lam lives on in weddings, festivals and religious ceremonies. Savannakhet is considered the heartland of modern mol lam.

05 The Secret War (1964–1973)

Laos sufrió la campaña de bombardeos más intensa per cápita en la historia de la humanidad: 270 millones de bombas de racimo lanzadas por Estados Unidos entre 1964 y 1973 como parte no declarada de la Guerra de Vietnam. Hoy, el 30% de esas bombas no explotó — y siguen matando personas. La Plain of Jars en Xieng Khuang, con sus misteriosas urnas de piedra de 2,000 años de antigüedad, es también una de las zonas más contaminadas por UXO (munición sin explotar) del mundo. Visitar el COPE Centre en Vientián es una experiencia que cambia la perspectiva.

Economy

A Country in
Transformation

Laos has experienced sustained economic growth since opening to the market in the 1990s. Its economy is built on hydroelectricity — exported mainly to Thailand and China — as well as mining, tourism and agriculture.

Laos–China Railway

Opened in 2021, the high-speed train linking Boten (on the Chinese border) with Vientiane in 3.5 hours has transformed the country's connectivity and accelerated regional trade.

Asia's Battery

With over 70 hydroelectric dams operating and 100 more under construction, Laos aims to become Southeast Asia's leading clean energy exporter by 2030.

Debt to China

Laos's external debt exceeds 120% of its GDP, with China as the main creditor. Managing this debt is the country's greatest economic challenge in the coming decade.

Lao Table

Flavours of
the Mekong

Lao cuisine is perhaps the least known in Southeast Asia — and for that reason, the most authentic. The umami depth of padek (fermented fish paste), the heat of chillies and the freshness of wild herbs define every dish.

🍚

Khao Niao

Sticky glutinous rice — the foundation of every Lao meal. Eaten with the hands, rolled into small balls and dipped into sauces.

🥗

Larb

The national dish. Minced meat (buffalo, pork or chicken) with fresh herbs, lime juice, chillies and ground toasted rice.

🥘

Or Lam

A Luang Prabang stew with buffalo, wild eggplant, wood herb and lemongrass. Slow-cooked with deep, complex flavours.

🥖

Khao Jee

The Lao baguette — French colonial heritage filled with pâté, chillies and fresh herbs. An accidental but perfect fusion.

🍜

Khao Piak Sen

Laos's answer to Vietnamese pho — fresh rice noodles in chicken or pork broth, thicker and more comforting.

🍺

Beerlao

The most celebrated lager in Southeast Asia. Golden, smooth and ubiquitous — the unofficial national symbol of Laos.
