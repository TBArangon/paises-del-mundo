Four Destinations

Places that
define Laos

From golden UNESCO temples to karst river valleys and megalithic mysteries — four places, four completely different Laos.

🛕 Luang Prabang — main image
Temples, monks, Mekong at dawn
01 / 04

Luang Prabang

UNESCO World Heritage City

Ancient royal capital where French colonial streets meet 30 golden Buddhist temples. The pre-dawn monk alms ritual (tak bat) is one of Asia's most moving experiences.

📅 Best time to visit
November — March
Dry, cool and clear. April's Lao New Year adds colour.
Tak Bat Kuang Si Falls Mt. Phousi Night Market Wat Xieng Thong Pak Ou Caves
💧 Kuang Si Falls / Night Market
02 / 04

Vang Vieng

Karst paradise on the Nam Song River

Dramatic limestone towers rising from rice paddies, turquoise lagoons hidden inside caves, and a river made for kayaking and drifting.

📅 Best time to visit
October — April
Dry season keeps rivers manageable and skies clear.
Tubing Blue Lagoon Tham Poukham Cave Hot Air Balloon Kayaking
03 / 04

Plain of Jars

UNESCO Megalithic Mystery

Over 2,000 stone jars — some 3 metres tall — scattered across the hills of Xieng Khouang. Origins unknown. Declared UNESCO World Heritage in 2019.

📅 Best time to visit
November — April
Pack a warm layer — mountain nights can drop to 5°C.
Site 1 — Thong Hai Hin Phonsavan UXO Museum Hmong Villages
04 / 04

Vientiane

Asia's most laid-back capital

A city that moves at its own gentle pace — French baguette bakeries beside golden stupas, riverside cafés spilling onto the Mekong promenade. The That Luang stupa is the national soul.

📅 Best time to visit
November — February
That Luang Festival (November) is unmissable.
That Luang Patuxai Arch Wat Si Saket Mekong Promenade Buddha Park Talat Sao Market

Climate

When to go

☀️
Dry Season
November — April
Clear skies, 20–30°C. Peak for travel. December–January nights in the north can drop to 5°C.
🌧️
Wet Season
May — October
Monsoon rains, lush green landscapes, fewer tourists. Some mountain roads become impassable. September brings the Mekong to its peak.
🌸
Peak Season
December — February
Perfect weather, cultural festivals, maximum visibility. Book accommodation early — especially in Luang Prabang.
🌅 Seasonal landscape — river / mist / rice fields

Travel Tips

Before you go

🛂
Visa
Visa on arrival (~$35, 30 days) or e-Visa online. Available at Vientiane & Luang Prabang airports.
💰
Budget
$30–50/day covers comfortable guesthouses, local food and transport. USD and Thai Baht widely accepted.
🛕
Temple etiquette
Cover shoulders and knees. Remove shoes before entering. Observe tak bat from a respectful distance in silence.
🚌
Getting around
High-speed train Vientiane–Luang Prabang in ~2h. Slow boats on the Mekong are an experience in themselves.
🏥
Health
Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations recommended. Drink bottled water only. Medical facilities limited outside Vientiane.
⚠️
UXO Safety
Laos is the most bombed country per capita in history. Never leave marked trails in rural areas, especially Xieng Khouang.
📡
Connectivity
Local SIM (Laotel / Unitel) ~$5 for 10GB. Good Wi-Fi in guesthouses and cafés in main cities.
🌿
Responsible travel
Support local businesses. The Elephant Conservation Center is the ethical standard for wildlife encounters.
